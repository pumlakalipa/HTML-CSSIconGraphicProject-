Poverty Status of Racial and Ethnic Populations in Manhattan Community District 7 Over The Last 12 Months

By Pumla Kalipa

Graphic Credit: Angelina Bambina, iStock by Getty Images

The number of employment in the United States economy dropped dramatically by the end of the 2020 fiscal year compared to previous years as the country battled with the COVID-19 pandemic. According to research conducted by the Urban Institute, 12% of volunteers who participated in the December 2020 Census Bureau Household Pulse Survey reported to not have had enough to eat, while one-fifth of renters reported being behind on their rent amid the pandemic. The institution projected a poverty rate of 7.7% for 2021. The projected poverty rate was much higher for Black non-Hispanic people, Hispanic people, non-Hispanic Asian American people and Pacific Islanders than for White, non-Hispanic people.

This report will document the poverty status of different racial and ethnic populations in Manhattan Community District 7 which include: the Upper West Side, Manhattan Valley, and Lincoln Square, over the last 12 months.

6.5% of the 144,955 White alone population in the District have an income below poverty level. An estimated 93.5% have an income at or above the poverty level.

33.2% of the 12,525 Black or African American alone population reported to have an income below the poverty level, while 66.8% of the population has an income at or above the poverty level.

10.8% of the 18,999 Asian alone population have an income that falls below the income poverty level. An estimated 89.2% of the population is projected to have an income at or above the poverty level.

18.0% of the 27,704 Hispanic or Latino population have an income that is below the poverty level. The remaining 82.0% of the population have an income at or above the poverty level.

13.9% of the 5,912 population that fall under Two or More races have an income below the poverty level. 86.1% of the population have an income at or above the poverty level.

21.5% of the 8,584 population that fall under the Other Race Alone category have an income below the poverty level, while 78.5% of the population have an income at or above the poverty level.
